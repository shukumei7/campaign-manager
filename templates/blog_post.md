# MirrRd Blog Post Spec
*Template for mirrrd.com/blog content | Updated: 2026-03-15*

---

## Purpose

mirrrd.com blog posts are an SEO content channel. Their job is to:

1. Rank for celebrity style search queries (e.g., "Jacob Elordi haircut 2026," "how to dress like Margot Robbie")
2. Deliver genuine style value so visitors stay and trust the site
3. Convert readers into mirrrd.com users via a contextual CTA

Every post must accomplish all three. A post that ranks but has no CTA is wasted traffic. A post with a CTA but thin content will not rank.

---

## Post Types

### Type A: Red Carpet / Event Look
*Use for: specific event appearances, premiere looks, awards show outfits*

Focus on the specific outfit, hair, and grooming from that event. Do not pad with generic fitness advice.

**Structure:**
1. Intro — the event, the look, why it matters (2–3 paragraphs)
2. Hair breakdown — specific cut, technique, product used (if known)
3. Grooming/makeup breakdown — skin, brows, lips/beard
4. Outfit breakdown — specific pieces or aesthetic, how to replicate the silhouette
5. Product CTA (see below)
6. Source links

**Length:** 600–900 words

---

### Type B: Style Evolution / Aesthetic Guide
*Use for: "Miley Cyrus at 33," "Jacob Elordi's old Hollywood style" — broader analysis*

Cover the full aesthetic across multiple domains. The 4-domain structure (Hair, Grooming, Clothing, Fitness) is appropriate here because the goal is an actionable replication guide.

**Structure:**
1. Intro — who they are, what makes their current aesthetic worth analysing (2–3 paragraphs)
2. Hair — cut, colour, maintenance, specific product recommendations
3. Grooming — skin routine, facial hair, brows
4. Clothing — aesthetic label, key pieces, specific brands at accessible price points
5. Fitness — only if it genuinely informs the look (e.g., physique is central to the celebrity's aesthetic). Skip if it would feel forced.
6. Product CTA (see below)
7. Source links

**Length:** 900–1,400 words

---

### Type C: Evergreen Celebrity Style Guide
*Use for: SEO-targeted pages, e.g., "How to look like Ryan Gosling," "Hailey Bieber aesthetic guide"*

Highest-effort format. Designed to rank long-term for "[celebrity] style" queries.

**Structure:**
1. Intro — the celebrity, their signature aesthetic, what makes it aspirational (2–3 paragraphs)
2. Full 4-domain breakdown (Hair, Grooming, Clothing, Fitness) — detailed, product-specific
3. "How to get started" section — 3-step actionable summary
4. Product CTA — strongest version (see below)
5. FAQs (3–5 questions Google might surface)
6. Source links

**Length:** 1,200–2,000 words

## Required: Factual Accuracy Checks

Before publishing any post, verify:

- [ ] **Celebrity name** — spelled correctly, confirmed real person with public profile
- [ ] **Celebrity age** — calculated from confirmed birth year, not guessed
- [ ] **Event name** — must be a real, verifiable event (Golden Globes, SAG Awards, Cannes, BAFTA, Met Gala, etc.). Do not use vague or invented names like "Actor Awards" or "Style Awards"
- [ ] **Event date** — confirm the event actually occurred and the celebrity attended
- [ ] **Celebrity credits** — any films, shows, or roles mentioned must be real
- [ ] **No duplicate posts** — check existing content before creating a new post on the same person/event

**Rule:** If you cannot verify a specific event claim, remove the event reference. Write "a recent red carpet appearance" rather than name a fake ceremony.

## Required: Product CTA

Every post must include a contextual call-to-action linking to mirrrd.com. Place it after the main content, before source links.

### CTA Copy (choose the most natural fit)

**For celebrity goal posts:**
> *Want to see what [Celebrity]'s style would look like on you? Upload a selfie at [mirrrd.com](https://mirrrd.com) — the AI analyses your features and builds a personalised plan covering hair, grooming, clothing, and fitness. Your photo never leaves your device.*

**For red carpet / event look posts:**
> *Inspired by this look? Try [mirrrd.com](https://mirrrd.com) — name [Celebrity] as your style goal and get a specific plan tailored to your own features. Free to try, no account needed.*

**For style evolution posts:**
> *Curious how this aesthetic translates to your face and build? [mirrrd.com](https://mirrrd.com) takes your selfie (analysed entirely in your browser — never uploaded) and generates a personalised roadmap: exact haircut, grooming routine, clothing direction, and fitness focus.*

**Do not:**
- Use generic "check out mirrrd.com" copy
- Place the CTA at the very top of the post
- Use urgency tactics ("limited time," "act now")

## Duplicate Prevention

Before writing a new post, check:

1. Is there already a post about this celebrity? (`/blog` index)
2. Is there already a post about this specific event/look?
3. Would this post cannibalize search rankings of an existing post?

**Rule:** One post per celebrity per event. If a celebrity needs both a red carpet post AND a style guide, they must have clearly different titles, different target keywords, and substantially different content.

## SEO Requirements

- **Title format:** `[Celebrity Name]'s [Specific Hook]: [Benefit or Descriptor]`
  - Good: "Demi Moore's SAG Awards 2025 Look: The Cropped Bob Redefining Beauty at 63"
  - Bad: "Demi Moore at 63: The Iconic Cropped Bob Redefining Beauty" (too vague on event)
- **Target keyword:** Include the celebrity's full name + the event or year in the title and first paragraph
- **Meta description:** 150–160 characters, include celebrity name + year + mirrrd.com brand name
- **Internal links:** Link to at least one other mirrrd.com blog post where relevant

---

## Tone & Voice

- Editorial, not promotional. Write like a style journalist, not a marketing copywriter.
- Specific over general. "A low taper fade with 3 inches of textured volume on top, styled with matte clay" beats "a modern haircut."
- Empowering, not judgmental. MirrRd is about closing the gap, not ranking people.
- Avoid: looksmaxxing terminology (mog, SMV, frame, hardmax), attractiveness scoring language, cosmetic procedure recommendations.

---

## Source Requirements

- Link to at least one credible source for any event-specific claim (entertainment news, Getty red carpet coverage, official event site)
- For product recommendations: link to the brand or retailer
- Do not cite sources that require login or paywalls

## Content Policy Compliance (Blogger / General Platform Standards)
*Updated: 2026-03-16*

These rules apply to all posts and must be respected by both the generator and any manual edits.

### Deceptive Content — Prohibited
- **No fabricated event names.** Use the exact name from source articles. If unknown, write "a recent red carpet appearance." Never invent a ceremony name.
- **No fabricated attributed quotes.** Do not put words in real people's mouths. Paraphrase in editorial voice instead.
- **No fabricated statistics or timeframes.** Do not state "first appearance in X years" or similar unless the source explicitly provides the figure.
- **No false factual claims** about a celebrity's age, credits, or attendance at events without source verification.

### Health & Appearance Claims — Use Hedged Language
- Frame all recommendations as suggestions, not guarantees: "try," "consider," "can help achieve" — not "will give you," "guarantees," or "results in."
- No medical claims. Style advice only (haircuts, grooming, clothing, fitness approach). Do not recommend cosmetic procedures or medical interventions.
- Do not imply that following the guide will definitively change someone's attractiveness outcomes.

### Celebrity Content — Editorial Only
- Posts must be clearly third-person editorial analysis, never written as if by the celebrity or as if the celebrity endorses mirrrd.com.
- Discussing a celebrity's public appearance and style is permitted editorial content.
- **Celebrity images:** If photos are ever added to posts, they must be properly licensed (Getty, official press releases, Creative Commons). Never use unlicensed celebrity images.

### Commercial CTAs — Permitted With Conditions
- Product CTAs linking to mirrrd.com are allowed. Posts must also deliver genuine style value — a post that exists solely to promote the product is not acceptable.
- CTAs must not be deceptive. Do not imply the product does something it doesn't.

### AI-Generated Content
- No platform prohibition on AI-generated content. However, AI output must pass the same factual accuracy standards as manually written content. Fabricated facts are a policy violation regardless of how the content was produced.

- [ ] No near-duplicate post already exists on this person/event
- [ ] Post type selected and structure followed accordingly
- [ ] Product CTA included with correct copy
- [ ] At least one source link for event claims
- [ ] Title contains celebrity full name + year or event
- [ ] Fitness section included only if genuinely relevant to the aesthetic
- [ ] Tone is editorial, not templated/robotic
- [ ] No fabricated event names, quotes, or statistics
- [ ] All appearance claims use hedged language (no outcome guarantees)
- [ ] Written in third-person editorial voice (not as or for the celebrity)
